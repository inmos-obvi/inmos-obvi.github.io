The paper <a href="https://doi.org/10.1029/2024GB008185"><strong>Offset Between Profiling Float and Shipboard Oxygen Observations at Depth Imparts Bias on Float pH and Derived pCO<sub>2</sub></strong></a>, led by **Seth Bushinsky (PI)**, has been published in *Global Biogeochemical Cycles*. The study compares Argo profiling float oxygen measurements with quality-controlled shipboard observations and finds a small but systematic oxygen offset at depth. These offsets can propagate into float pH adjustments and derived surface pCO<sub>2</sub>, affecting estimates of important ocean carbon system variables. The results highlight the importance of careful oxygen calibration for biogeochemical floats and for interpreting long-term changes in ocean oxygen and carbon chemistry.
